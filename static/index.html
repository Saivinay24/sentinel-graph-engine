Our Solution

We ask a different question.

Every other approach compares you to yourself. We compare you to everyone who operates like you.

The Problem

68% of breaches use valid credentials. The attacker has the right username, the right password, accesses permitted resources, and looks completely normal. No rule breaks. No alert fires. Current tools were never built to catch this.

  • Rule-Based

    "Does this violate a policy?"

    Valid credentials, valid permissions — policy says allow. 87% of attacks stay within bounds and are never caught.

  • ML / UEBA

    "Is this different from what YOU normally do?"

    Insiders shift their own baseline slowly. New employees have no history. 5% FPR floods SOC with thousands of daily false alerts.

  • Our Approach
    Sentinel-Graph

    "Is this normal for people like you?"

    GraphSAGE builds behavioral peer groups from the identity graph. Every login is scored against that group, not just personal history.

Where the gap shows up

Three attacks that expose the blind spot

Lateral movement within policy

Valid credentials, valid permissions. Rules allow it. But none of the user's HR peers ever touch that finance resource. Flagged.

New employee account takeover

No personal history to baseline against. Traditional ML is blind from day one. Sentinel-Graph uses the peer group immediately.

Slow privilege creep

Insider expands access over months. Their baseline shifts with them. Their peer group does not. The deviation compounds and fires.

How It Works

Five signals. One score. One decision.

Every login triggers all five signals simultaneously. They fuse into a single risk score and an adaptive decision in under 15ms.

  • 25% weight · Key signal

    Peer Deviation

    Cosine distance between the user's GraphSAGE embedding and their community centroid. Fires when behavior drifts from what their functional peer group does — regardless of the user's own history.

  • 25% weight

    Geo-Velocity

    Haversine distance between consecutive logins divided by elapsed time. Flags physically impossible travel — Paris to New York in two hours means one of those logins is not the real user.

  • 20% weight

    Temporal Anomaly

    Z-score deviation from the user's typical login hour distribution. A 2 AM login from an account that has never been used outside business hours is a strong signal on its own.

  • 15% weight

    Device Trust

    Penalises unregistered or new devices based on the user's device history. A login from a device the account has never used before gets a significantly higher risk contribution.

  • 15% weight

    Entity Affinity

    Tracks first-time access to sensitive resources. The first time any user touches a high-value resource — a production database, a finance system — the risk contribution spikes until a pattern is established.
